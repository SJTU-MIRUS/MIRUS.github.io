The Marine Intelligent Robotics and Unmanned Systems Laboratory (MIRUS) is dedicated to breaking through key technologies in the fields of deep-sea exploration, marine resource development, environmental monitoring, and marine ranching. With intelligent perception, autonomous decision-making, collaborative operation, and environmental adaptation as its core driving forces, the laboratory conducts systematic and forward-looking research and development focused on the following four key directions:

### 1. Advanced Navigation and Control Methods for Marine Robotics

- **Advanced Navigation and Control Methods for Marine Robotics**
  - Develop high-precision, high-robustness navigation and positioning technologies for complex marine environments (strong disturbances, weak communication, low visibility), including multi-source information fusion, terrain-aided navigation (TAN), and SLAM.
  - Develop robust adaptive control algorithms to enhance motion control performance (trajectory tracking, station keeping, path planning, disturbance and current rejection) of robots in highly nonlinear, time-varying, and uncertain marine environments. Simultaneously, explore AI-based (deep learning, reinforcement learning) intelligent autonomous control strategies to achieve task-driven adaptive behavioral decision-making.

- **Homogeneous/Heterogeneous Marine Robotic Collaboration and Game Theory**
  - Research distributed collaborative perception, task planning, formation control, and autonomous cooperation mechanisms for multi-AUV/USV/UAV systems (homogeneous or heterogeneous). Simultaneously, develop efficient communication networking technologies (acoustic, optical, cross-medium) to ensure reliable information exchange for collaborative systems under complex sea conditions.
  - Explore intelligent game-theoretic strategies for marine robotic swarms, applied to mission execution in adversarial environments (e.g., area denial, attack-defense confrontation), enhancing system survivability and mission success rate in dynamic adversarial scenarios.

- **Embodied Intelligence and Digital Twin for Marine Robotics**
  - Promote the development of “embodied intelligence” in marine robotics, deeply coupling the robot's physical form, perception capabilities, and motion characteristics with its intelligent decision-making, enabling it to understand and adapt more naturally to complex marine physical environments.
  - Construct high-fidelity digital twin systems for marine robotics and their operating environments, enabling real-time state mapping, perception-driven operation, fault diagnosis, virtual testing, and training.

- **Novel Concept and Biomimetic Marine Robotics**
  - Design and develop novel-concept marine robotic platforms with innovative configurations, new driving and propulsion methods (e.g., biomimetic propulsion, soft actuation), and special functions (e.g., adhesion capability, cross-medium navigation).
  - Conduct in-depth research on the motion mechanisms, perception modes, and environmental adaptation strategies of marine organisms (e.g., fish, cetaceans, manta rays, octopuses), and develop high-performance biomimetic marine robotics.

This laboratory aims, through the interdisciplinary integration and collaborative innovation in the aforementioned frontier directions, to overcome bottlenecks in individual intelligence and group collaboration of marine robotics, enhancing their autonomy, adaptability, cooperativity, and operational effectiveness in extreme and complex environments. It strives to provide an “intellectual engine” for humanity to gain a deeper understanding of the ocean and harness its potential.
